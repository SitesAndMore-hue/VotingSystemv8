How Websites Track You & How to Protect Yourself

Cookies & Local Storage

Websites store small data files (cookies) on your device to remember your preferences, login status, and track your activity across sessions and sites.

Protection: Regularly clear cookies, use browser's private mode, or install cookie-blocking extensions.

Browser Fingerprinting

Websites collect dozens of data points about your browser and device configuration to create a unique "fingerprint" that can identify you across different sites.

Protection: Use privacy-focused browsers like Firefox with resistFingerprinting enabled, or Tor Browser.

IP Address Tracking

Your IP address is logged with every website visit and can reveal your approximate location, internet provider, and be correlated across different sites you visit.

Protection: Use a reputable VPN service to mask your real IP address from websites.

Privacy Protection Tips

Immediate Actions

  • • Use browser extensions like uBlock Origin, Privacy Badger, or DuckDuckGo Privacy Essentials
  • • Enable "Do Not Track" in your browser settings (though many sites ignore it)
  • • Clear cookies and browsing data regularly
  • • Use private/incognito browsing mode for sensitive activities

Long-Term Strategies

  • • Consider using privacy-focused browsers like Firefox, Brave, or Tor
  • • Use a reputable VPN service for everyday browsing
  • • Switch to privacy-focused search engines like DuckDuckGo or Startpage
  • • Review and adjust privacy settings on all your online accounts
