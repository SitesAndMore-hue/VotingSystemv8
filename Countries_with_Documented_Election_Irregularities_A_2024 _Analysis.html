Countries with Documented Election Irregularities: A 2024 Analysis

Examining 13 nations with evidence of electoral manipulation and what we can learn about voting integrity

Published: December 2024 • Independent Research • Documented Evidence

Free and fair elections are the cornerstone of democratic societies, yet numerous countries continue to face allegations of electoral manipulation and irregularities. This comprehensive analysis examines 13 nations where documented evidence suggests election processes have been compromised.

Based on reports from international observers, academic research, and documented incidents, this ranking highlights countries where election integrity remains a significant concern. Understanding these cases helps illuminate the importance of transparent voting systems worldwide.

Documented Evidence of Election Irregularities

1

Azerbaijan's Premature Results

In the 2013 presidential election, Azerbaijan's state news agency reportedly released official election results before voting had concluded. This incident raised serious questions about the integrity of the counting process and whether results were predetermined.

2

Russia's Statistical Anomalies

Analysis of Russian election data by academics and watchdogs has revealed statistically improbable patterns, including round-number turnout figures and suspicious distributions of votes for the ruling party that suggest manipulation.

3

Belarus Internet Shutdown

During the 2020 presidential election, Belarus authorities shut down internet access across the country, preventing independent monitoring and coordination of election observation. This blackout coincided with widespread reports of ballot stuffing.

4

Venezuela's Candidate Disqualifications

In multiple elections, Venezuelan authorities have disqualified leading opposition candidates on questionable grounds, effectively preventing genuine political competition. These actions have been widely condemned by international observers.
